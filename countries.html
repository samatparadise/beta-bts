Alignment Overview

Each ASEAN state is assessed across three dimensions: security relationships (military alliances, intelligence sharing, defence procurement), digital infrastructure (which bloc's cloud, 5G, and surveillance technology dominates), and governance norms (which bloc's legal and regulatory model shapes domestic digital law). Alignment scores reflect a synthesis of these dimensions — not a single measure. They are analytical assessments, not official designations, and they change over time. US-Aligned: Strong security ties and dominant Western digital infrastructure.   Hedge: Active dual-engagement with both blocs; maintains strategic ambiguity.   CN-Aligned / Dependent: Chinese infrastructure dominance, governance norm convergence, or structural debt dependency.
