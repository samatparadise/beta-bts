What this is

Between Two Stacks is a prototype research and advocacy tool for digital rights researchers, policy makers, and activists working on tech accountability and tech justice issues in Southeast Asia. It is currently in beta and actively seeking feedback from the communities it is intended to serve.

It maps how US and Chinese technology blocs are reshaping surveillance, data governance, platforms, AI deployment, cybersecurity, financial technology, digital trade law, and soft power across the ten ASEAN member states — with consistent dual-bloc analysis rather than framing one superpower as the problem and the other as the solution.

Data is indicative and drawn from public sources. All claims are referenced. Everything should be verified before use in published work. The site will be updated as new research becomes available and as contributors add to it.

This project exists in conversation with a growing body of investigative and research work mapping tech-capital power — including The Authoritarian Stack (authoritarian-stack.info), Citizen Lab's systematic spyware documentation, Access Now's Digital Security Helpline research, and Freedom House's annual Freedom on the Net reports.

Built with AI assistance

This prototype was researched, structured, and built with the assistance of Claude Sonnet 4.6, Anthropic's AI assistant. The research framework, dual-bloc analysis, operator profiles, country assessments, and site architecture were developed in collaboration with Claude over an extended working session. All factual claims have been cross-referenced against primary sources and should be independently verified.

Using AI assistance for a project of this kind raises its own accountability questions — consistent with the site's values, we disclose it plainly. The analytical judgements, editorial decisions, and framing are human; the research assistance, drafting, and code generation are Claude's.

Methodology

Domains were identified through a systematic review of literature from Citizen Lab, Access Now, Article 19, Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, CSIS, the Wilson Center, MERICS, and Freedom House, focusing on domains where US and Chinese tech blocs have documented and distinct impacts on rights and governance in ASEAN.

Actor selection focuses on companies, agencies, and institutions with significant documented presence in ASEAN — either through contracts, infrastructure, platform usage, or governance influence. It is not exhaustive. Actors were selected for their significance to rights and governance outcomes, not market share alone.

Country alignments are analytical assessments based on publicly documented policy positions, infrastructure contracts, intelligence relationships, and trade agreements — not official designations. They reflect a snapshot and should be treated as dynamic.

References are drawn from peer-reviewed research, NGO investigations, investigative journalism, and policy analysis from established institutions. All URLs were active as of the dataset compilation date. Dead links should be replaced with archived versions (archive.org) or equivalent sources.
